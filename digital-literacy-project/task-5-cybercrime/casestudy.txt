UPI fraud is a common cybercrime where users are tricked into approving fake payment requests. Fraudsters often pretend to be buyers, sellers, or bank officials and send a “collect request” on apps like Google Pay or PhonePe.

Many users think they are receiving money and approve the request without checking carefully. Instead of receiving money, they end up sending it.

Students and elderly people are common targets because they may not fully understand how digital payments work.

The consequences include financial loss, stress, and risk of personal data misuse. In many cases, recovering money becomes difficult if the fraud is not reported immediately.

This shows the importance of being aware and cautious while using online payment systems.
